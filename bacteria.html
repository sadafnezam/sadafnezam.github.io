Bacteria

Sep 20244-Member TeamUnity 2D

Narrative Designer · Level Designer

An experimental game where players live the final moments of the last dying bacterium on a lab plate.

Playable Build GitHub

This game was an experiment to create an emotional experience of resignation and inevitability, so it is more narrative-driven rather than mechanical. There is no conventional winning condition in this game, and players learn from the beginning that they can't change the outcome, no matter how they engage.

It is portraying the final moments of a last dying bacterium, as a result of discovery of a new cure. The narrative unfolds through a monitor recording and auto-transcribing a discussion between a scientist and his assistant. Through their dialogue, we learn that the experiments were all successful and they were able to eliminate all the bacteria using this new discovery. The scientist explains how these new generation of nano-cells that reproduce rapidly, spread everywhere, and leave no room for the bacteria.

As the scientist describes this process, you're living it. You explore the petri dish and encounter a few nano-cells. They seem defeatable at first, but they multiply just as described. You soon realize that the combat is pointless. Every stat is intentionally unbalanced against you. Attacks drain your energy, your health is decreasing even while standing still because you are poisoned, and every encounter accelerates the decline.

Death is inevitable. You can fight hopelessly, or stand still and let your health drain naturally, or ignore the enemies and explore the map instead, and enjoy your last moments.

At one point, you'll find another dying bacterium, too weak to move. You can share part of your health with him, and he'll follow you as a companion. Elsewhere, there's a spot with a pillow to sit on and take in the view together until the end comes. After you die, one final line appears: the scientist asking his assistant to clean up the plate.

This is a project where narrative was directing all the decisions. Mechanics, resources, and gameplay loop were all built around the intended experience. If I want to be super honest, this is the kind of games I'd love to focus on, if I had all the free time and resources on the planet. Although I'm not very proud of the quality of final execution, I like the idea behind it.
