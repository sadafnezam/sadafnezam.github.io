The Up-Grader

Spring 2025Course project5-member teamUnity

Gameplay Programmer · System Designer · Level Designer

A 2D Metroidvania platformer where you play as a broken little robot looking for its missing pieces to build itself back together, but every upgrade comes at a cost.

itch.io GitHub

The focus of this project was designing an interconnected progression and economy system. The character is a small robot missing most of its parts, and it rebuilds itself by taking in each piece it finds. Every weapon and ability is a modular piece that can be attached, detached, or swapped at any time, and this modularity is what the whole upgrade and inventory system is built on.

You start with a basic weapon, limited movement, and access to only a small part of the map. From there, the progression builds through a few connected systems:

  • Abilities: each ability stone gives you a new movement ability. You need to explore to find them.
  • Map: each new ability opens up new parts of the map and lets you climb higher.
  • Enemies: as you progress further, enemies grow stronger in both movement freedom and attack patterns, and bigger enemies are more likely to drop better loot.
  • Weapons: loot is randomly either a weapon or a buff. Stronger weapons let you take down tougher enemies and reach further.
  • Code stations: these are checkpoints scattered across the map. You always respawn at the last one you checked in at, and each check-in stacks a debuff on the final boss. It's up to the player whether to speedrun straight to the boss or take the time to explore and weaken it first.

The level is designed in a way that you always need to go up. The player often catches glimpses of what's available above, which encourages them to keep climbing. Higher areas require abilities you don't have yet, so progression is gated by your abilities unlocked. The final boss sits at the highest point of the map.

The player inventory works on a slot tradeoff system, where health acts as the anchor resource that every other resource is tied to. In the inventory, equipping any item such as weapons, abilities, or buffs takes up part of the health.

The boss runs on the same rule: checking in at each code station takes up part of the boss's health, and if you check in at all of them, the boss is down to 50% health by the time you reach it.
