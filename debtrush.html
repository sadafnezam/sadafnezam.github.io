Debt Rush

Spring 2026Capstone project3-member teamUnity

Developer · Designer · UX Research

A co-op top-down action game where two partners manage a shared budget to survive the debt collectors and escape the city.

itch.io GitHub Demo

You and your partner took out loans for your dream business, it failed, and now the debt collectors are at your door, chasing you everywhere. The only way out is to fight your way to the next city while keeping enough money on hand to buy your escape ticket.

The focus of this project was designing a co-op system where players have to communicate and coordinate well to succeed, both in making financial decisions together and in fighting off their enemies.

These are the core elements of the game:

  • Shared Budgeting: every level starts with a Shop Phase where both players spend from one shared pool of money to build their loadout, and nothing is final until both players agree. This is where players negotiate and make decisions together. Then they move into the Play Phase, combat and exploration using only the items they bought.
  • Asymmetric Roles: Player 1 is offensive, with weapons for direct combat. Player 2 is defensive and support, with shields and ranged support items. They have to work together to beat the level.
  • Hidden Individual Objectives: at the start of each level, each player privately picks a secondary goal, sometimes complementary, sometimes directly contradicting their partner's. You don't find out what your partner was actually optimizing for until the level ends. This adds a light layer of competition within the players' cooperation.

A dynamic split-screen camera keeps both players on one shared view when they're close and splits when they separate, so each player keeps the autonomy to explore or fight on their own without losing the other.

The first level is split into two regions: a suburban area and the main city. The suburbs act as a tutorial space, a scaled-down version of the whole game, so players learn every mechanic before reaching the city.

We playtested Debt Rush with player pairs and used surveys to rate the experience across a set of qualities, such as ease of control, enjoyment, goal clarity, feedback, challenge, and immersion. This let us identify where the game was falling short: goal clarity and in-game feedback scored the lowest. We used those results to guide the next iteration, and made changes to address them:

  • Highlighted key information in dialogue so players know the objective.
  • Added arrows pointing to each player's partner, making them easier to find.
  • Upgraded the UI to give more legible feedback.
  • Added more color variety across city buildings to help with navigation and orientation.
