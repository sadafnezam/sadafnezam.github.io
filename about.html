About Me

I'm a game developer working across programming and design, mainly in game engines Unity/Unreal, building gameplay systems, game AI, player mechanics, and designing levels and the core gameplay loop.

I'm a recent graduate in Game Science and Design from Northeastern University, Boston, MA. My undergraduate background was in film studies with a screenwriting concentration, but I also had an interest in computer science and programming on the side, and was learning on my own. When I first learned Unity as a side hobby, I realized game development is where all of my scattered interests, like art, storytelling, tech, math, and computer science, collide with each other. I enjoy bringing ideas to life through engineering and design, and crafting memorable, engaging interactive experiences. I like solving the problems that sit between code and design, making a system that's technically solid and also feels good to play.

Outside of work and games, I'm usually side-questing through life, gamifying things to keep myself motivated, from how I structure my daily routine to picking intriguing routes for my running. I'm also often building stories in my head, with detailed plots, dialogue, and complex characters inspired by people in my real life.
